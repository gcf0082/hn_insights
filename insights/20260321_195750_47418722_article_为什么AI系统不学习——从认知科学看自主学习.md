## Summary

Current AI systems lack the ability to learn autonomously after deployment. The paper proposes a three-system architecture inspired by human cognition:

- **System A** — Learning from observation (self-supervised learning)
- **System B** — Learning from action (reinforcement learning)
- **System M** — Meta-control orchestrator that dynamically switches between A and B

The paper identifies three roadblocks: fragmented learning paradigms, outsourcing of learning to humans, and lack of scalable architecture-building methods. A potential solution involves an evolutionary-developmental (evo-devo) bilevel optimization framework.

## Key Findings

1. **Diminishing returns on LLM scaling**: Even architects like Ilya Sutskever and Andrej Karpathy suggest hyperscaling is hitting limits — data wall, lack of environment interaction, language-centrism, no continual learning.
2. **System A (Observation)**: Learns from passive sensory data via SSL; good for representation but disconnected from action, struggles with causality.
3. **System B (Action)**: Learns through trial-and-error; sample-inefficient, needs well-specified rewards, hard in open-ended domains.
4. **Bidirectional A↔B synergy**: A provides representations, world models, intrinsic rewards to B; B generates task-relevant data and grounding for A.
5. **System M (Meta-Control)**: Acts as a central orchestrator monitoring meta-states (prediction error, uncertainty, arousal) and outputting routing meta-actions — analogous to prefrontal cortex.
6. **Evo-Devo framework**: Use bilevel optimization over simulated environments to jointly learn the meta-control policy and initial states, bootstrapping robust real-world behavior.
7. **Higher learning modes**: A fixed System M enables advanced modes like learning through communication and imagination — only found in large-brain species.

## Three Roadblocks Identified

| # | Roadblock | Proposed Solution |
|---|-----------|-------------------|
| 1 | Fragmented learning paradigms | Unified System A + B framework |
| 2 | Externalization of learning to humans | System M meta-control architecture |
| 3 | No effective method to build at scale | Evolutionary-developmental bilevel optimization |

## Relevance to Current AI

The paper bridges AI and cognitive science, arguing that autonomous learning is essential for reliable real-world AI deployment. The proposed A-B-M architecture parallels LeCun's earlier "A Path Towards Machine Intelligence" and Dupoux's cognitive science roadmap for reverse-engineering infant learning.
